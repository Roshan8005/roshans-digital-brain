# 🩻 Radiology & Medical Imaging Basics
Location: Occipital Lobe (Visual Processing Center)

## 1. Fundamentals of Medical Imaging
Medical imaging allows visualization of the internal structures of the body to diagnose, monitor, or treat medical conditions.

### A. Radiodensity (How tissue appears on X-Rays/CTs)
* **Radio-opaque (White)**: High-density tissues (e.g., bones, calcifications, metallic implants) block radiation.
* **Radiolucent (Black)**: Low-density areas (e.g., lungs filled with air, gas in the bowel) allow radiation to pass through.
* **Soft Tissues (Gray)**: Intermediate density (e.g., muscles, liver, kidneys, fluid collections).

## 2. Key Modalities
### A. Projectional Radiography (X-Ray)
* **Description**: Uses a single beam of electromagnetic radiation to produce a 2D projection.
* **Best Used For**: Bone fractures, chest infections (pneumonia), dental exams, and mammography.
* **Common Views**: Posterior-Anterior (PA), Anterior-Posterior (AP), Lateral.

### B. Computed Tomography (CT Scan)
* **Description**: Uses rotating X-ray beams and digital detectors to compile a series of 2D slices into a 3D volume.
* **Best Used For**: Acute trauma, cerebral hemorrhage, pulmonary embolism, cancer staging, and abdominal pain.
* **Contrast Agents**: Iodine-based contrast is often injected IV to highlight blood vessels and organs.

### C. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)
* **Description**: Uses strong magnetic fields and radiofrequency pulses to excite hydrogen protons in the body, measuring their relaxation times. Does not use ionizing radiation.
* **T1-Weighted vs. T2-Weighted**:
  * **T1**: Fat is bright (hyperintense), fluid/CSF is dark (hypointense). Excellent for anatomy.
  * **T2**: Fluid/CSF is bright, pathology/edema is bright. Excellent for detecting inflammation or lesions.
* **Best Used For**: Brain tumors, spinal cord injuries, ligaments/tendons (soft tissues), and joint imaging.

### D. Ultrasonography (Ultrasound)
* **Description**: Uses high-frequency sound waves to produce real-time dynamic images. Safe for pregnancy (no radiation).
* **Best Used For**: Obstetrics, gallbladder evaluation, cardiac imaging (Echocardiogram), and deep vein thrombosis (DVT).

---

## 3. Anatomical Planes
* **Axial (Transverse)**: Cuts the body into upper (superior) and lower (inferior) sections. Viewed from the patient's feet looking up.
* **Sagittal**: Cuts the body into left and right sections.
* **Coronal**: Cuts the body into front (anterior/ventral) and back (posterior/dorsal) sections.
