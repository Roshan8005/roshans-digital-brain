## 1. Core Cryptographic Primitives
Blockchain networks rely heavily on mathematics and cryptography to ensure security, immutability, and trustlessness.
* **Hash Functions (e.g., SHA-256)**: Take any input and produce a fixed-size 256-bit string. It is one-way (cannot reverse-engineer input) and collision-resistant. Used for block validation (mining) and address generation.
* **Asymmetric Cryptography (Public-Key Cryptography)**:
  * **Private Key**: A secret 256-bit number used to sign transactions (proves ownership). Keep safe!
  * **Public Key**: Derived mathematically from the private key (using Elliptic Curve Cryptography, e.g., secp256k1). Used to verify signatures.
  * **Address**: A hashed representation of the public key (e.g., `0x...` in Ethereum).

---

## 2. Distributed Ledger & Consensus Algorithms
A blockchain is a decentralized database shared across a network of nodes. To agree on the state of the ledger, nodes use consensus mechanisms:
* **Proof of Work (PoW)**: Nodes (miners) compete to solve a complex mathematical puzzle. The first to solve it proposes the next block and earns a reward. Highly secure but energy-intensive. Used by Bitcoin.
* **Proof of Stake (PoS)**: Validators are chosen to propose blocks based on the number of coins they lock up (stake) as collateral. Faster, scalable, and energy-efficient. Used by Ethereum.

---

## 3. Smart Contracts & Decentralized Applications (dApps)
* **Smart Contracts**: Self-executing code stored on the blockchain that automatically executes agreements when predefined conditions are met. (Written in languages like **Solidity** for Ethereum, or Rust for Solana).
* **EVM (Ethereum Virtual Machine)**: The runtime environment that executes smart contracts across all nodes in the Ethereum network.
* **ERC Standards**:
  * **ERC-20**: Standard for fungible tokens (tokens where each unit is identical, e.g., USDT, LINK).
  * **ERC-721 / ERC-1155**: Standards for Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) representing unique digital assets.

---

## 4. Decentralized Finance (DeFi) & Web3 Concepts
* **Liquidity Pools**: Crowdsourced pools of tokens locked in smart contracts used to facilitate trading on decentralized exchanges (DEXs like Uniswap) without traditional market makers.
* **Gas Fees**: The transaction fee paid to validators/miners to compute transactions. Paid in the network's native currency (e.g., ETH on Ethereum).
* **Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs)**: Cryptographically secured identities owned entirely by the user instead of centralized servers.
