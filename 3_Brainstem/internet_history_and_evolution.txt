# 🌐 The Internet: History, Discovery & Evolution
Location: Brainstem (Communication Relay Center)

## 1. The Discovery & Birth (1960s)
* **Precursor**: ARPANET (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network), funded by the U.S. Department of Defense in 1969.
* **The First Message**: On October 29, 1969, the first message "LO" was transmitted from UCLA to Stanford Research Institute. The intended word was "LOGIN," but the system crashed after the first two letters.
* **Key Innovation**: Packet Switching (developed by Paul Baran and Donald Davies), which broke data into small packets sent across variable routes, making networks robust against physical cuts.

---

## 2. Evolution of Protocols
* **1970s - TCP/IP**: Vinton Cerf and Robert Kahn designed Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) and Internet Protocol (IP), defining how packets are assembled, addressed, routed, and received. TCP/IP became the standard on January 1, 1983, marks the official birthday of the Internet.
* **1989 - The World Wide Web (WWW)**:
  * **Inventor**: Sir Tim Berners-Lee at CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research).
  * **Innovation**: Invented HTTP, HTML, URL, and the first web browser, turning a scientific text network into a graphical, linked system for the public.

---

## 3. Technology Generations & Future
* **Web 1.0 (Static)**: Read-only pages (1990s). Users could read text but not write or interact (e.g., directory portals).
* **Web 2.0 (Social & Interactive)**: Read-write web (2000s-present). Users became creators, enabled by databases, AJAX, and cloud hosting (e.g., YouTube, Facebook, SaaS, Google Docs).
* **Web 3.0 (Decentralized & Semantic)**: Read-write-own web. Focuses on decentralization, blockchain ledgers, sovereign identity, and smart data processing using AI.
* **Modern Infrastructure**: Fiber-optic submarine cables, 5G wireless networks, edge computing caches, and globally distributed CDNs (Content Delivery Networks).
